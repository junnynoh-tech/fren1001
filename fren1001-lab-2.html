Ma Famille

📝Summary & Context

In this lab you will talk about your family — introducing and describing family members using adjectives, ages, and -ER verbs.

Record a 2-minute video introducing your family. Show a family photo or draw a family tree. Describe at least three family members and yourself.

🚦Process
  1. 1Study the vocabulary cards and grammar below.
  2. 2Practice speaking out loud — many times!
  3. 3Record via Zoom or phone (MP4 or MOV).
  4. 4Upload to your D2L Assignment Folder.

📂 Click Lab 2 in your D2L Assignment Folder to submit.

📦What You Will Need
  • 🎥 Zoom or phone/computer
  • 🎞️ MP4 or MOV format
Click to enlarge
📖Grammar Review
✅Requirements
  • 2 minutes long
  • Face visible
  • Include a picture of your family or a family tree drawing
  • Brief self-introduction (greeting, name, age)
  • Describe at least 3 family members: hair, eyes, 3 adjectives
  • Include ages (use avoir: Mon père a 50 ans.)
Ask two questions to your audience in French. Example: Combien de frères et sœurs avez-vous ?
Incorporate family vocabulary, descriptive adjectives, and -ER verbs from class.
🚫 No online translator or AI allowed

Conjugating -ER Verbs
The most common verb group in French — drop -er, add the endings below
parler — to speak
Jeparle
Tuparles
Il / Elle / Onparle
Nousparlons
Vousparlez
Ils / Ellesparlent
aimer — to like / love
J'aime
Tuaimes
Il / Elle / Onaime
Nousaimons
Vousaimez
Ils / Ellesaiment
💡 Rule: The -e, -es, -e, -ent endings are all silent! Only -ons and -ez are pronounced differently. This is why listening and speaking practice matters so much.
