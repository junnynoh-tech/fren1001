🎯What these labs are for

These lab assignments are designed to improve your listening and speaking skills in French. Each lab gives you a structured context to practice vocabulary, grammar, and authentic communication — building toward real conversational fluency.

📋All submissions require
  • 📹Your face must be visible in every video submission.
  • ⏱️Each video must be at least 2 minutes long (Lab 1: 1.5–2 minutes).
  • 🎬Record using Zoom, your phone, or any tool that exports MP4 or MOV.
  • 📤Upload to your D2L Assignment Folder by the due date.
  • 🔇Record in a quiet location with clear audio.
  • 🚫No online translators, AI tools, or scripts. Use class notes and your textbook only.

Key Points to Remember

  • 🕐No late submissions. Assignment folders close at the due date and time. Contact the lab before the due date if you have technology issues.
  • 📅Don't wait until the last minute. Review each lab's vocabulary and grammar early — practice out loud many times before recording.
  • 📖Only approved resources. You may use class notes, your textbook, and WordReference.com. Do not read from a prepared script during recording.
  • 🚫Academic misconduct. Using online translators, AI tools, or outside help is considered academic misconduct and may result in serious consequences.
  • 📬Check your email. If due dates or instructions change, you will be notified by email. It is your responsibility to check regularly.

Grading

Lab assignments are graded by Language Lab staff. If you have questions about a grade, contact the Language Lab directly — not your course instructor.

Occasionally due dates or instructions may change. Always check your UNG email and D2L announcements.
