🎯What these labs are for

These assignments are designed to improve your listening and speaking skills in French. Each lab gives you a real-world context to practice the language you've learned in class. You can complete all labs at home via D2L.

🎬Activity type — Summer Session
☀️ All labs in this session are individual (solo) activities. Practice many times on your own before recording your final video. There are no partner labs.

🙋 All Labs — Individual Activities

Record on your own. Practice multiple times before your final recording. Labs are due on Wednesdays at 11:59 PM — summer moves fast, so start early!

📋All submissions require
  • 📹Your face must be visible in every video submission.
  • 🎞️Upload as MP4 or MOV to the D2L assignment folder.
  • 🔇Record in a quiet location with clear audio.
  • 🌐Use Zoom or another tool that exports the correct file format.

Summer Session Lab Schedule

1
Tout sur moi
📖 Unité 1 🙋 Individual ⏱ 2 min+

Introduce yourself: greetings, name, age, nationality, personality adjectives, and reasons for studying French. Ask 2 questions.

2
Ma Famille
📖 Unité 2 🙋 Individual ⏱ 2 min+

Introduce your family using a photo or family tree. Describe at least 3 members with physical descriptions, adjectives, and ages.

3
La Maison
📖 Unité 3 🙋 Individual ⏱ 2 min+

Give a video tour of your room or house. Describe furniture using adjectives, colors, and prepositions. Talk about 3–4 household chores.

4
Révision Complète
📖 Révision Labs 1–3 🙋 Individual ⏱ 2 min+

Watch all review videos in D2L, then create a 2-minute video covering topics from Labs 1–3. Answer at least 5 review questions and ask 2 questions to your professor.

Key Points to Remember

  • 🕐No late submissions. Assignment folders close at 11:59 PM on Wednesdays. Contact the Lab before the due date if you have technology issues.
  • 📅Start early. Summer sessions move fast — review each lab's vocabulary and grammar early and plan ahead.
  • 📖Only approved resources. Class notes, your textbook, and WordReference.com. Do not read from a prepared script during recording.
  • 🚫Academic misconduct. Using online translators, AI tools, or outside help may result in serious consequences.

Grading & Feedback

Your course instructor does not grade lab assignments. They are graded exclusively by Language Lab staff. Grading typically takes about one week. If you have questions about a grade, contact the Language Lab directly — not your professor.

Occasionally, due dates or instructions may change. The Lab will notify students via email. It is your responsibility to check your UNG email account regularly.
